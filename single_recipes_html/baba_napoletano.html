How to make Baba' napoletano

Baba napoletano is a traditional Italian dessert made with a soft and fluffy cake that is soaked in a sweet syrup. The cake is typically flavored with rum or other liqueurs, and is often served with whipped cream or fresh fruit. The dessert is believed to have originated in Naples, Italy, and has since become a popular treat throughout the country. It's a delicious and indulgent dessert that is perfect for special occasions or as a sweet ending to a meal. Baba napoletano can come in various sizes, ranging from small bite-sized portions to larger cakes that can serve multiple people. Its soft and moist texture, along with the rich flavors of the syrup and liqueurs, make it a favorite among dessert lovers. Whether enjoyed alone or paired with your favorite toppings, baba napoletano is a classic Italian dessert that is sure to delight your taste buds.

prep time

1 h.

cook time

25 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Desserts

instructions

step 1

Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, mix together the yeast, warm water, and 1 tsp of sugar. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy.

step 2

Prepare the dough: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and remaining sugar. Add the yeast mixture, eggs, milk, butter, and vanilla extract to the bowl. Mix with a wooden spoon or a dough hook until everything is well combined.

step 3

Knead the dough: Transfer the dough onto a floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

step 4

Let the dough rise: Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover it with a damp cloth. Let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours or until it doubles in size.

step 5

Bake the baba' part(1): Preheat the oven to 350°F. Grease a large baking dish or a baba' mold with butter. Punch down the dough and divide it into small pieces. Roll each piece into a ball and place it in the mold, making sure they are evenly spaced. Cover the mold with a damp cloth and let it rise for another 30-45 minutes.

step 6

Bake the baba' part(2): Bake the baba' for 20-25 minutes or until they are golden brown. Remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.

step 7

Soak the baba': In a small saucepan, heat the rum and some sugar over low heat until the sugar dissolves. Using a toothpick, poke several holes in the baba' and brush them with the rum mixture. Let them soak for about 10-15 minutes.

step 7

Serve and enjoy: Dust the baba' with powdered sugar and serve them warm or at room temperature. You can also add some whipped cream and fresh berries on top, if desired.

ingredients

250g flour

50g sugar

50g butter, softened

10g active dry yeast

50ml warm milk

1/4 tsp salt

Grated zest of 1 lemon | 50g raisins (optional) | 50g candied fruit (optional)

1/4 cup rum or marsala (optional)

Note that you can customize the ingredients based on your preferences or dietary restrictions.

tools

Large mixing bowl

Measuring cups and spoons

Electric mixer or whisk

Pastry brush

Small saucepan

Baba' molds (if using) | Cooling rack

Baking sheet or muffin tin (if not using molds)

Clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap

Note that some of these tools may be substituted with other kitchen tools you already have at home.
