How to make Carbonara

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with spaghetti, eggs, Pecorino cheese, black pepper, and guanciale (or bacon). The sauce is made by whisking together raw eggs and grated cheese, which is then cooked by the heat of the pasta. The result is a creamy and rich sauce that coats the pasta, with the bacon adding a salty and savory flavor. It's a simple and delicious dish that is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or a special occasion.

prep time

30 min

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Main Pasta Dishes

instructions

step 1

Cook the pasta: Boil a pot of salted water and cook spaghetti (or other pasta of your choice) until al dente. Reserve some pasta water before draining.

step 2

Prepare the sauce: While the pasta is cooking, fry some diced pancetta (or bacon) in a pan until crispy. In a mixing bowl, whisk together eggs, grated parmesan cheese, black pepper, and a bit of the reserved pasta water. Once the pasta is cooked and drained, add it to the pan with the pancetta and toss to coat the pasta with the fat.

step 3

Combine the pasta and sauce: Remove the pan from the heat and pour the egg mixture over the pasta. Quickly toss the pasta until the sauce is evenly distributed and the eggs have cooked from the residual heat, creating a creamy sauce. Add more pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce. Serve immediately with additional grated parmesan cheese and black pepper on top.

ingredients

Spaghetti or pasta of your choice (around 400g)

Guanciale or pancetta (100g)

Eggs (3)

Pecorino Romano cheese, grated (100g)

Black pepper, freshly ground (to taste)

Salt (to taste)

tools

Large pot for boiling pasta

Colander to drain pasta

Large skillet or pan for cooking the sauce

Mixing bowl

Whisk for mixing the eggs and cheese

utting board and knife for chopping garlic and parsley (optional)
